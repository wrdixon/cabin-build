Building Systems

Roof Assembly

Layer order from rafters up: Rockwool, ZIP sheathing, ice & water shield, 2″ polyiso, vertical strapping, standing seam metal. Venting strategy TBD.

Windows & Doors

Marvin Elevate windows throughout. Door RO must be enlarged before exterior work. Window schedule needs verification against Marvin order confirmation.

Siding

Vertical white cedar shiplap over a rainscreen assembly: 2″ polyiso continuous, two layers of PT 1×3 furring strips.

Flooring

Material TBD — solid pine preferred for feel, but engineered may suit unheated periods better. Source: local mill near Jamaica VT (TBD).

Loft

Sleeping loft over east end, approximately 8 ft deep, full width of cabin. Loft joists may serve as rafter ties — structural detail to be confirmed.

Rafter Ties

None installed yet. Required for structural integrity of cathedral ceiling. Loft joists are a candidate; confirmation needed before closing roof.

Wood Stove

Model TBD, no electricity required. Makeup air kit required to prevent depressurization in tight envelope. Chimney routing (wall vs. roof) undecided.

Pile Bracing

Corner helical pile(s) need angled bracing for wind resistance using angle irons. Bill's scope to source and install.

Electrical

Sub-panel fed from garage sub-panel, approximately 150 ft run. Hiring licensed electrician. Panel size TBD. Circuits: heat pump, fan, lighting, outlets.

Ventilation

Panasonic WhisperGreen bath fan (40–50 CFM) plus passive makeup air inlets (product TBD). Critical in tight envelope to avoid depressurization.

Heat Pump

9,000 BTU mini-split as primary heat alongside wood stove. Electrical circuit required. Backup heat for periods without wood stove use.

Key Cross-System Dependencies

Dependencies

The door rough opening must be enlarged before the siding and exterior work sequence can begin. Rafter ties must be resolved — likely through the loft joist connection — before the roof assembly is closed out. The wood stove makeup air requirement directly drives ventilation product selection, and chimney routing affects roof assembly detailing. Electrical panel sizing depends on the final load of the heat pump, bath fan, lighting, and any stove accessories. Flooring selection is independent but should be confirmed before interior finish work begins.
