Ventilation In Progress

Exhaust-only mechanical ventilation — passive makeup air inlets — dedicated outside air kit for wood stove

Overview

The cabin will be a tight envelope, so mechanical ventilation is required to maintain air quality. The strategy is a single exhaust fan with passive makeup air inlets — appropriate for a small, intermittently occupied space. The wood stove requires a dedicated outside air kit to prevent depressurization of the envelope when the fan is running.

Decisions

1. Exhaust fan

Panasonic WhisperGreen, 40–50 CFM.

Reason: ASHRAE 62.2 calculation for this space yielded approximately 30 CFM required. WhisperGreen at 40–50 CFM provides adequate margin, is quiet, reliable, and energy efficient. An ERV or HRV is not warranted for a space this small with intermittent occupancy.

2. Makeup air strategy

Passive inlets only — no powered makeup air. Products under consideration: Aldes Fresh-80, Lunos Nexxt, Smartvent/ProVent.

Reason: exhaust-only systems at this CFM level create modest negative pressure that passive inlets can offset. Wood stove makeup air is handled separately via outside air kit — passive inlets are for general ventilation only.

3. Wood stove outside air kit

Dedicated outside air kit (OAK) required for the wood stove. Product TBD — must be compatible with stove model once selected.

Reason: a tight envelope with the exhaust fan running creates risk of depressurization and backdraft at the stove. The OAK supplies combustion air directly to the firebox, isolating it from the living space. Not optional in this assembly.

Installation Notes

  1. Locate and cut passive inlet penetrations through siding, furring, polyiso, and ZIP before siding is installed. Cap and flash at exterior. Coordinate locations with Siding.
  2. Install WhisperGreen fan in bathroom ceiling or wall. Duct to exterior — keep duct run short and straight where possible.
  3. Install OAK per wood stove manufacturer instructions. Combustion air duct runs from exterior directly to the firebox area — does not mix with living space air.
  4. Run fan electrical circuit to panel. See Electrical.
  5. After envelope is closed, conduct blower door test before interior finish work begins to verify tightness.
