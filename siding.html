Siding In Progress

Vertical white cedar shiplap on double-layer PT 1×3 furring rainscreen over 2″ polyiso — immediate next phase

Overview

Vertical white cedar shiplap on a rainscreen over 2″ polyiso continuous exterior insulation. The rainscreen provides a drainage and drying plane between the siding and the insulation layer. Polyiso and strapping go on next — this is the immediate next phase of the build. Door rough opening enlargement must happen first. Rockwool installation in wall bays is independent of exterior work and can happen at any time — before, during, or after exterior is complete.

Decisions

1. Cladding material

6″ nominal sound tight knot kiln-dried rough-sawn white cedar shiplap sourced from Kerber mill, Guilford VT.

Reason: locally sourced, good performance in Vermont climate, consistent with aesthetic of other buildings on the property. Rough-sawn face, not surfaced.

2. Orientation

Vertical installation.

Reason: sheds water well, works with vertical furring rainscreen, consistent with the board profile.

3. Wall stack order (outside in)

White cedar shiplap → two layers PT 1×3 furring → 2″ polyiso → ZIP sheathing (already installed) → 3.5″ Rockwool between studs → 2×4 studs at 16″ OC.

Reason: continuous exterior insulation eliminates thermal bridging through studs. Double furring layer creates adequate rainscreen cavity depth. Vertical furring aligns to studs for structural fastening.

4. Furring layout

Vertical furring aligned to every stud (16″ OC). Horizontal cross-furring at 16″ OC across wall height of approximately 9′6″ on the main walls.

Reason: vertical furring carries load and creates drainage plane. Horizontal cross-furring provides nailing surface for vertical siding at code-required intervals.

5. End grain treatment

Outlast Q8 on cedar end cuts. Copper Green Brown on PT furring cuts.

Reason: end grain is the primary moisture entry point on cedar. Sealing cuts extends life significantly. PT cuts need separate treatment appropriate to treated lumber.

6. Face nailing

Cedar face-nailed through furring into studs.

Reason: shiplap profile and vertical orientation make blind nailing impractical. Face nailing is standard for this application.

Installation Notes

  1. Confirm door rough opening is enlarged before any exterior work begins. This is a hard blocker. See windows-doors.html.
  2. Rockwool installation in wall bays is independent of exterior work and can happen at any time — before, during, or after exterior is complete.
  3. Install 2″ polyiso over ZIP sheathing. Tape all seams. Treat all end cuts with compatible tape or sealant. After polyiso is installed, secure temporarily with plastic cap nails or cap staples to hold flat before furring goes on. These are not structural.
  4. Install vertical PT 1×3 furring over polyiso aligned to studs at 16″ OC. Fasten with 5″ or 6″ GRK RSS or Headlok structural screws driven into studs. Minimum 1.5″ penetration into stud, 2″ preferred. These screws carry the full weight of the siding assembly — do not substitute shorter or lighter fasteners. Treat cut ends with Copper Green Brown before installation.
  5. Install horizontal PT 1×3 furring at 16″ OC across wall height. Fasten to vertical furring with 3″ GRK RSS or equivalent epoxy-coated structural screws. Do not use nails for this connection — screw withdrawal strength is required. Treat cut ends.
  6. Cedar is acidic and will corrode standard steel fasteners over time. Anywhere cedar and PT lumber are in contact, all fasteners must be stainless steel or hot-dipped galvanized. Electroplated zinc fasteners are not acceptable in this assembly.
  7. Install bug screen or closure strip at base of rainscreen cavity before siding.
  8. Install windows and door before siding. Flash all openings per window manufacturer requirements. See windows-doors.html.
  9. Treat all cedar end cuts with Outlast Q8 before installation.
  10. Face-nail cedar shiplap with stainless steel ring-shank nails. Do not use electroplated zinc or standard galvanized — cedar acidity and PT contact require stainless or hot-dipped galvanized minimum. Maintain consistent reveal.

If unavailable, belly band at 9′ plate line is the fallback.

  • Bottom of wall detail: confirm bug screen or closure at base of rainscreen cavity.
  • Polyiso tape product: confirm tape is compatible with polyiso facing before ordering.

Dependencies

Related Systems

Windows & Doors — flashing and rough opening details must be resolved before siding install. Door RO enlargement is a hard blocker for all exterior work.

Roof Assembly — polyiso and strapping at roof edge need to coordinate with wall polyiso termination and fascia detail.

Ventilation — passive air inlet locations must be set before siding goes on. Penetrations through siding and furring must be planned in advance.

Wood Stove — if chimney exits through wall, penetration must be planned and roughed in before siding.
