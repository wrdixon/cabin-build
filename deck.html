Deck In Progress

8×8 ft south-facing platform with stairs, three sonotube footings, ledger connection to cabin wall

Overview

A small deck on the south face of the cabin, to the right as you exit the door. Approximately 8×8 ft platform with stairs descending to grade. Three sonotube footings on poured concrete bases below frost line provide support for both the deck platform and the stair landing. Ledger connection to the cabin wall requires careful flashing to prevent moisture intrusion into the wall assembly.

Decisions

1. Size and layout

8×8 ft deck platform to the right of the door as you exit. Stairs descend from the deck to grade.

Reason: functional landing and sitting area without dominating the south face.

2. Stair width

48″ minimum stair width.

Reason: allows two people to pass comfortably. 36″ is code minimum for single-person passage but too narrow for comfortable two-person use.

3. Footings

Three sonotube footings on poured concrete bases below frost line (48″ minimum, confirm with Dummerston — may be deeper at 1,200 ft elevation). Footing layout: two at outer corners of deck, one at stair landing where stringers meet grade.

Reason: stairs that are not footed heave and separate over time. Three footings handle deck and stair loads.

4. Excavation

Hire neighbor with mini excavator to dig footing holes. Pour flared concrete base at bottom of each hole, set sonotube on top, pour column.

Reason: Vermont soil at 48″+ depth is unpredictable and rocky. Power auger risks getting stuck. Mini excavator can deal with rocks and gives more control.

5. Ledger flashing

Standoff spacers between ledger and wall assembly, metal flashing lapped behind siding and kicked out over ledger face, visible gap between siding bottom and ledger top.

Reason: trapped moisture behind a ledger is the primary rot mechanism on decks. Standoff spacers eliminate the trapped moisture problem entirely. Visible gap confirms drainage is working.

Installation Notes

  1. Confirm frost line depth with Dummerston before digging. Do not assume 48″ is sufficient at this elevation.
  2. Dig three footing holes with mini excavator. Pour flared concrete base at bottom of each hole. Set sonotube on poured base, plumb, and brace. Pour concrete column.
  3. Work out ledger attachment detail before siding goes on the south wall. Ledger flashing must integrate with the wall assembly at this stage — it cannot be added cleanly after siding is installed. See siding.html.
  4. Install standoff spacers between ledger and wall. Confirm visible gap at top of ledger before siding is installed above.
  5. Flash ledger top with metal flashing lapped behind siding, kicked out over ledger face.
  6. Install deck framing on posts.
  7. Install decking with appropriate gap for drainage.
  8. Install stairs with stringers bearing on footed landing. Do not rest stringers on bare grade.
  9. Install railings if required by height and permit.
