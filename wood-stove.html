Wood Stove In Progress

Jøtul F 602 V3 as primary heat source — outside air kit required — no electricity needed

Overview

The Jøtul F 602 V3 wood stove serves as the primary heat source alongside the mini-split heat pump. No electricity required — the stove operates independently during power outages. The tight, well-insulated envelope requires a dedicated outside air kit (OAK) to supply combustion air directly to the firebox and prevent depressurization when the exhaust fan is running. Stove placement must be finalized before chimney or OAK work can begin.

Decisions

1. No electricity required

Stove must operate without electricity.

Reason: resilience during power outages. Heat pump fails without power; wood stove provides independent backup heat with no electrical dependency.

2. Stove model

Jøtul F 602 V3.

Reason: rated up to 800 sq ft, which fits the 480 sq ft footprint with margin. Jøtul Fusion catalytic technology allows clean burning at very low output — critical for a tight, well-insulated space where minimum heat output matters as much as maximum. Emissions 1.40 gr/hr. No electricity required. Outside air kit available (Jøtul #156408). 6″ flue collar, top or rear exit.

3. Outside air kit

Jøtul #156408 required.

Reason: tight envelope with exhaust fan creates depressurization risk and potential backdraft at stove. OAK supplies combustion air directly to firebox from exterior, bypassing living space. Not optional in this assembly.

Installation Notes

  1. Finalize stove placement before any chimney or OAK work begins. All downstream work depends on stove location.
  2. Sleeve and flash OAK wall penetration (4″) through wall assembly before polyiso and siding are installed. See siding.html.
  3. Install stove on non-combustible hearth pad. Confirm hearth pad dimensions and material per Jøtul clearance requirements before ordering.
  4. Install class A chimney pipe at 6″ flue collar. See chimney.html for full chimney system details.
  5. Connect OAK conduit from exterior wall cap to stove firebox area per Jøtul #156408 instructions. Install rodent screen at exterior cap.
  6. Confirm all combustible clearances per Jøtul installation manual before first fire.

Dependencies

Related Systems

Chimney — full chimney system details including roof penetration, class A pipe spec, and flashing are on chimney.html. Stove location must be finalized before chimney work begins.

Siding — OAK wall penetration must be sleeved and flashed before polyiso and siding are installed. This is a sequencing blocker.

Ventilation — OAK is separate from the passive inlet system. Exhaust fan and OAK must be coordinated to ensure the envelope is not depressurized when the stove is in use.

Heat Pump — wood stove and heat pump are the two heat sources. Wood stove handles cold periods and power outages; heat pump handles moderate temperatures.
