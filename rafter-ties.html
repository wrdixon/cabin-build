Rafter Ties Open

Cathedral ceiling lateral bracing — engineering confirmation required — loft joists candidate at east end

Overview

The cabin has a cathedral ceiling with no rafter ties currently installed. Rafter ties are required to resist outward thrust on the walls from roof loads. The loft joists may serve this function at the east end of the cabin if the joist-to-rafter connection is adequate; ties are still required across the remainder of the roof span regardless.

Decisions

1. Loft joists as rafter ties at east end

The loft joists at the east end of the cabin may serve as rafter ties if the joist-to-rafter connection detail is adequate. This is a candidate approach, not a confirmed decision — engineering confirmation required before relying on it.

Reason: if confirmed, this eliminates separate ties at the east end and integrates structure with the loft floor. Needs engineering sign-off before proceeding.

  1. Confirm loft joist structural role before planning tie layout for the remainder of the roof. Do not assume loft joists are adequate without engineering confirmation.

  4. Install before interior ceiling work begins. Confirm timing with Forest.
