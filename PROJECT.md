# Jamaica Cabin -- Project Reference

## Overview
16x30 ft stick-built cabin on helical piles, approximately 2 feet above grade. Southern Vermont (Dummerston/Jamaica area), Zone 6, approximately 1,200 ft elevation. South-facing. Permit issued through Dummerston -- may need renewal as it is approximately 2 years old.

- **Lumber supplier:** Ryan, LaValley's
- **Local mill (flooring):** TBD, near Jamaica VT

## Structure
- Foundation: helical piles
- Framing: 2x4 studs at 16" OC, 9 ft plate height
- Rafters: 2x10 at 24" OC into a 2x12 ridge board
- Cathedral ceiling throughout, loft over east end (~8 ft long, full width)
- Loft joists may serve as rafter ties -- structural confirmation needed
- No rafter ties currently installed -- to be added
- Corner pile(s) need angled bracing for wind resistance -- angle irons, Bill's scope

## Envelope
- Walls: 2x4 studs, 3.5" Rockwool in bays, ZIP sheathing, 2" polyiso continuous exterior, rainscreen (two layers PT 1x3 furring), vertical white cedar shiplap
- Roof: 2x10 rafters, Rockwool in bays, ZIP sheathing, ice and water shield full coverage, 2" polyiso, vertical strapping (Headloks at 24" OC into rafters), standing seam metal
- ZIP sheathing on walls and roof already installed
- Next phase: polyiso, strapping, siding, windows, door
